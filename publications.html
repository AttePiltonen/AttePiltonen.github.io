Beyond Hard-Core Bosons in Transmon Arrays

Our publication presents a framework for studying the effective dynamics of highly excited states of coupled transmons using perturbation theory. This allows us to describe collective phenomena in a unified and accurate manner, and opens up opportunities to explore additional lattice models beyond the standard Bose-Hubbard model.

Composite bosons in transmon arrays: numerical approach

This talk for the March Meeting of the American Physical Society 2022 discusses the many-body effects in numerical simulations of the Bose-Hubbard model using capacitively coupled transmons, which offer a means of analog quantum simulation. We explore these effects within experimentally realistic parameter ranges and timeframes limited by the onset of decoherence and dissipation, focusing on the interplay between on-site and off-site interactions.
