I am a data scientist who is passionate about using artificial intelligence and Python to solve complex problems.

Welcome!

As a physicist-turned-data scientist, I bring a unique perspective to my current work, which focuses on leveraging Python and Machine Learning to address challenges in geospatial intelligence and maritime domain awareness. I use my expertise in data analysis and modeling to extract insights from complex datasets and develop predictive models that support decision-making in these areas. Originally from Oulu, Finland 🇫🇮, I am currently based in Orlando, Florida ☀️🇺🇸.

I hold a Master's degree in Theoretical Physics from the University of Oxford, with specializations in Astrophysics and Quantum Field Theory. Since completing my Master's Thesis, I have conducted research as part of the Astrophysics Department at Oxford that centers on statistical analyses of the dynamics and structure of the ß Lyrae star system. To this end, I have leveraged high-quality spectroscopic data from our Global Jet Watch observatories.

My research interests also extend to the field of Quantum Computing, in which I have amassed extensive experience through my work with the Nano and Molecular Systems Research Unit at the University of Oulu. Specifically, I have developed numerical models for analyzing the quantum phase behavior and dynamics of superconducting qubits using Python. Additionally, I have designed algorithms to optimize self-correcting quantum logic states.
