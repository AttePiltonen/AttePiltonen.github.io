Beyond Hard-Core Bosons in Transmon Arrays

In this publication, we presented a framework for studying the effective dynamics of highly excited states of coupled transmons using perturbation theory. This allowed us to describe their collective phenomena in a unified and accurate manner, and opens up opportunities to explore additional lattice models beyond the standard Bose-Hubbard model. This research has applications in quantum analog simulations, a promising branch of quantum computing.

Published in Physical Review X Quantum, also available on arXiv.

Composite bosons in transmon arrays: numerical approach

This talk by my research team for the March Meeting of the American Physical Society 2022 discussed the many-body effects in numerical simulations of the Bose-Hubbard model using capacitively coupled transmons, which offer a means of analog quantum simulation. We explored these effects within experimentally realistic parameter ranges and timeframes limited by the onset of decoherence and dissipation, focusing on the interplay between on-site and off-site interactions.

Abstract available on the APS website.
